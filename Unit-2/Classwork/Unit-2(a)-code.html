Describe what $p$ is, and state any assumptions needed for the geometric distribution to be used as a model. Print a density histogram of x. Describe why the distribution looks the way that it does. In particular, why are there so few numbers greater than $5$ or $6$?

In [25]:
x = rgeom(500,0.5)
hist(x, prob = TRUE)

The geometric distribution represents the number of failures in a sequence of Bernoulli trials before success occurs. With a probability of success $p=0.5$, the probability that we get many failures (e.g., $>6$) will be relatively low.
